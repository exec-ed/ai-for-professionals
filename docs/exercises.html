The 4 Exercises

Each exercise builds on the previous one. You'll move from exploring AI tools to applying them to your own work.

Exercise 1: AI Tool Test Drive

Compare AI tools on a real workplace task

40 min

Give the same workplace scenario to ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Compare outputs for quality, tone, accuracy, and gaps. Discover which tools suit which tasks.

Framework: AI Task Suitability Spectrum

You'll need: Accounts on at least 2 AI platforms

Output: Personal notes on tool strengths and preferences

Exercise 2: Prompt Engineering Workshop

Build prompting skills through 3 progressive rounds

60 min

Choose from 6 workplace scenarios. Round 1: write a naive prompt and see what you get. Round 2: apply the RTCF framework and compare the difference. Round 3: try advanced techniques â€” chain-of-thought, iteration, multiple versions.

Framework: RTCF Prompt Framework

Scenarios: Difficult client email, policy summary, status update, interview questions, decision analysis, process document

Output: Personal prompt template library

Exercise 3: Data Analysis Challenge

Analyse real data with AI and produce a business brief

45 min

Upload a RetailFlow customer service dataset (260 tickets, 6 months) to your AI tool. Answer three business questions: What drives dissatisfaction? When do response times spike? What should the manager do? Verify the AI's findings.

Framework: AI-Assisted Analysis Workflow

Data: RetailFlow customer service CSV (provided)

Output: One-page insight brief for the customer service manager

Exercise 4: Your AI-Enhanced Workflow

Redesign a real task from your own job

40 min

Pick a task you do at least weekly. Map every step. Classify each step using the Opportunity Finder. Redesign the workflow with AI at the steps where it adds value. Test one step live.

Framework: AI Opportunity Finder

You'll need: A real recurring task from your job

Output: Before/after workflow diagram with at least one tested step

The progression is deliberate

Exercise 1 explores. Exercise 2 builds skill. Exercise 3 applies skill to data. Exercise 4 applies everything to your real work. By the end, you've moved from "trying AI" to "using AI at work."
